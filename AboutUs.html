About Us

We, Team Onyx, are a group of aviation enthusiasts who have a passion for all things related to the world of aviation. By participating in various aeromodelling competitions, we get to expand our pool of knowledge and test our technical, analytical skills by validating our research. The objectives help us understand the issues faced in the aviation industry, and get an insight on how to tackle them. Such opportunities expose budding engineers to real-life applications of the theory learnt within the four walls in a fun and challenging manner.

Every year, we build atleast two planes, where we apply our aerodynamic and structural skills, and precise manufacturing techniques to mould our design in reality for the competition. In addition to the competition’s aspect, we also take pride in being a part of a team and developing friendships. The team’s passion for all things related to aviation and desire to enrich student educational experience has brought us success.

Our Mission

“Aiming for the Sky!”

Our Mission is to improve our knowledge and skills with each passing day while simultaneously providing a foundation and helping other students to pursue their interest in the field of aviation.

Why join Team Onyx

Being a student run group, you can learn how to work within a team environment and conduct in a professional manner. Team Onyx provides opportunities of interaction with industry giants and veterans in the field through which you can further your own development. With Team Onyx you can learn all the intricacies and engineering challenges faced and learn to solve them in the most efficient manner.

This mega project is a valuable learning experience and a very rewarding extracurricular activity. Students here follow a design process similar to what is used in the engineering industry and it gives them a chance to develop practical engineering skills. At Team Onyx, we select students from all engineering fields. So if you have a passion for aviation, a love to design, build, and fly planes, or simply want to gain technical and soft skills that will look amazing on your resume, or all of the above, then this could be for you!

SPYTHUN-V


This aircraft was built to compete for SAE Aero design West 2012. The objective was to design an aircraft that could lift as much weight as possible while observing the available power and aircraft’s dimensional constraints. We fabricated a T-tail configuration aircraft that could provide good stability while being a rigid structure.

Achievements:

  1. Overall 25th
  2. 4th in India
  3. 16th in design report and technical presentation

ARYA


This aircraft was built to compete for SAE Aero design West 2013 with an aim to lift a maximum payload of 18 kg. A double fuselage structure was constructed which possessed a higher strength. Another innovation was the use of paper honeycomb structure to support the payload basket.

Achievements:

  1. 2nd best in India and 1st in Maharashtra
  2. 5th in technical presentation
  3. 24th in overall Competition

BATWING


This aircraft was built to compete for SAE Aero design West 2014 where we came up with an ingenious design which we had never been employed before. The design had swept back wings with a complete elimination of a fuselage. The plane was marginally stable and controls depending on the expertise of the pilot.

Achievements:

  1. 1st in Mumbai and Maharashtra
  2. 5th best Indian Team
  3. 25th in overall Competition
  4. 14th in Design Report

MILLENNIUM FALCON


The Millennium Falcon is the largest aircraft Team Onyx has built to date, with a wingspan of 144 inches to boast and the capability to lift upto 22 kgs weight. The Millennium Falcon was fabricated to compete in SAE International East, Texas, where it flew successfully and completed its course with a respectable flight score, which paralleled even the top teams in the world.

Achievements:

  1. 15th in the World
  2. 3rd in Asia Pacific
