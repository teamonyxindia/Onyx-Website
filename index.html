Who Are We?

We are Team Onyx India, a student-run, non-profit, Aero Design Club of K.J. Somaiya College of Engineering, Vidyavihar. We design and fabricate Remote-Controlled Aircrafts and compete in numerous Aero Design and Aero-Modelling competitions like SAE International and Boeing National Aero-Modelling Competition. We also conduct many events such as Multi-Drone, a drone racing competition and Aero-Modelling Workshops for students who are interested in the field of aviation. Our team not only focuses on popularizing the field of Aero-Modelling and providing a foundation for many other enthusiasts, but also to improve at different aspects of the aviation field and become better with each passing day.

Media and Event


Autodesk Industries held an event at CIDCO exhibition centre Vashi in 2016 where we displayed our model.


Abhiyantriki is K. J. Somaiya College’s annual technical fest which boasts participation from organizations like DRDO, ISRO, BARC and in 2017, Team Onyx showcased their model to a footfall of over 15000 people


MakerMela is India’s largest Maker movement. In their annual fest in 2018 at Somaiya Vidyavihar, Team Onyx again had its model up for display for thousands of people to see, observe and understand the processes behind making this plane.


Apart from showcasing our works in various events, Team Onyx has also organized a Drone Racing Competition in KJSCE for participants to test their piloting skills and win various cash prizes.

Workshops



The aeromodelling workshop organized by Team Onyx is one of the most anticipated events of the academic year. Usually a 2-Day workshop, students from various educational institutes across Mumbai participate to have a better understanding of concepts related to aviation and aerodynamics and apply the same concepts while making their own plane the following day taking into consideration various design elements and manufacturing processes.



This year we had conducted an Aero-Modelling Workshop where we witnessed the participation of over 100 students. They were taught all the basics and some advanced studies related to the field of aviation followed by a practical session of fabrication where they were asked to build an aircraft of raw materials and understand the intricacies of fabrication. A test flight session was also held to fly the aircrafts built by the students.
